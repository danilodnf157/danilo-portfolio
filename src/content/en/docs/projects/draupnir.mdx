    A modern, accessible, and high‑performance design system built with **React**, **TypeScript**, and **Tailwind CSS**. Inspired by the Norse ring that multiplies itself, Draupnir symbolizes **reusability** and **scalability** of components. It provides visual consistency, **design tokens**, **themes**, accessibility, and first‑class DX with **Storybook** documentation.

  <section>
    ## From inconsistency to a component platform

    ### Act 1 — The fragmented mosaic
    In multi‑team products — especially without UX/UI teams — UI drifts: mismatched colors, random spacing, broken behavior, and forgotten accessibility. As the front‑end owner, I saw that fixing case by case didn’t scale across teams.

    ### Act 2 — The insight
    Consolidate UI decisions into brand‑aligned **design tokens**; organize the ecosystem with **Atomic Design**; document with **Storybook**; enforce **accessibility**, **themes**, and **centralization** from day one to speed up all teams.

    ### Act 3 — The proposal
    A design system with:
    - Versioned **tokens** (colors, typography, spacing, shadows) ready for branding.  
    - **Atomic Design** (atoms → molecules → organisms) with cohesive APIs.  
    - **Accessibility** (ARIA, keyboard navigation).  
    - **Themes** (light/dark) and extensibility.  
    - **Documentation** in **Storybook**, usage guides and best practices.  
    - **Multi‑platform distribution** (web and mobile) with versioned packages.  
    - Strong **TypeScript** typing for DX and safety.

    ### Act 4 — Expected impact
    - Dramatically fewer visual inconsistencies.  
    - Faster screen delivery through real reuse.  
    - Fewer accessibility bugs.  
    - More predictable, coherent delivery across teams.  
    - Faster onboarding thanks to documentation and centralized standards.

    ### Act 5 — Next steps
    Expand the catalog (tables, data viz), publish packages, and migration guides across apps, including presets for mobile.
  </section>

    ## Highlights

    * **Design Tokens:** Centralized palette, typography, spacing, shadows.  
    * **Atomic Design:** Layers of components (atoms → molecules → organisms).  
    * **Accessibility:** ARIA support and keyboard navigation.  
    * **Themes:** Native light/dark and extensibility.  
    * **Responsive:** Components adaptable to different screens.  
    * **Performance:** Tree‑shaking and lean APIs.  
    * **TypeScript:** Strong typing end‑to‑end.

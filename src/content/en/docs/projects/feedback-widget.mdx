    ## From corporate pain to a proposed solution

    ### Act 1 — The dangerous silence
    In corporate routines, teams often spend long hours investigating bugs with too little information. Tickets arrive late and without visual context. The real pain isn’t the bug — it’s the silence.

    <Callout type="warning" title="Observed risk (personal experience)">
      Without contextual feedback, fixes become bets — costing time, focus, and user trust.
    </Callout>

    ### Act 2 — The insight
    What if users could tell the story at the exact moment of pain with an **automatic screenshot**? Turn frustration into direction, reducing noise during triage.

    ### Act 3 — The proposal
    An embedded, non‑intrusive widget with three pillars:
    - **Immediate context:** screenshot plus lightweight metadata.
    - **Near‑zero friction:** 3 feedback types (Problem, Idea, Other) submitted in seconds.
    - **Closed loop:** email notification and backend record for later analysis.

    ### Act 4 — Expected impact (hypothesis)
    No real clients yet; the goal is to validate these gains in a pilot:
    - **Time to understand a bug:** ↓ 60–80% with contextual screenshots.
    - **Triage cost per ticket:** ↓ 30–50%.
    - **Lead time (discovery → fix):** ↑ 2–3x.
    - **Actionable ideas/month:** consistent growth via simple categorization.

    <Callout type="info" title="Status">
      Proposal ready for a controlled PoC with clear measurement metrics.
    </Callout>

    ### Act 5 — Next steps
    - Routing: problems → engineering board; ideas → discovery backlog.
    - Thematic grouping: keywords and originating screen.
    - Mobile SDK (Expo) to broaden reach.
  </section>

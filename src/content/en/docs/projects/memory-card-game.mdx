    ## From a simple idea to a complete PWA memory game

    ### Act 1 — Choosing the right challenge
    I wanted an exercise that pushed reasoning without heavy backends. A memory game looked too simple — until I decided it would be a lab for UX, accessibility and PWA craft.

    <Callout type="warning" title="Hypothesis">
      If the game were fast, accessible and offered clear progression (achievements/modes), it could move beyond casual play and become engaging.
    </Callout>

    ### Act 2 — Design insight
    Turned “flipping cards” into micro‑decisions: pacing, reveal timing, audio/visual cues and a difficulty curve. The goal: sustain flow and avoid cognitive fatigue.

    ### Act 3 — Building
    - **Architecture:** React + TypeScript, Vite build, full PWA (install + offline).
    - **Game loop:** precise timing, progress persistence and 4 distinct modes.
    - **Accessibility:** full keyboard + screen reader support, managed focus and ARIA.
    - **Feedback:** synchronized animations and sounds for every critical event.

    ### Act 4 — Expected impact
    - Longer sessions thanks to achievements and mode variety.
    - Reduced rage‑quit due to clear cues and consistent pacing.
    - High replayability offline (PWA) — great for mobile use.

    <Callout type="success" title="Practical outcome">
      My second web game, consolidating accessible UX and front‑end engineering best practices applied to web‑native games.
    </Callout>

    ### Act 5 — What remains
    Beyond fun, a reusable scaffold to prototype web games focused on progression, performance and accessibility.
  </section>

  <section className="mt-6">
    ## Game Modes & Achievements

    - **Classic:** shuffled pairs, standard pacing.
    - **Time Attack:** countdown timer, bonuses for fast combos.
    - **Zen:** flow and precision, no time pressure.
    - **Local Duel:** two players on the same device.
    - **Achievements (examples):** “Flawless”, “Speedrunner”, “Sequence Master”, “Offline Marathon (PWA)”.

    ## From engineering pain to a practical proposal

    ### Act 1 — The invisible bottleneck
    In fullstack products, the front end often drifts into a “component monolith”: slow builds, tight coupling, duplicated code, and poor observability. Evolving features like “nearby places” becomes costly and imprecise.

    ### Act 2 — The insight
    Modularize by domain, apply real **code splitting**, and automate the repetitive. Standardize critical decisions (build, lint, deploy, metrics) so they’re predictable and traceable.

    ### Act 3 — The proposal
    A framework with:
    - **Modular architecture** and true **DRY** (shared components/utilities).
    - **Observability** from day one to reduce MTTR.
    - **Matrix CI/CD** and dependency automation for predictable releases.
    - **Haversine-based geolocation**, computing distances accurately with low cost.

    ### Act 4 — Expected impact
    - Lower TTFB and faster first interaction.  
    - Fewer regressions via duplication elimination.  
    - Safer, more frequent dependency updates.  
    - Traceable releases with actionable metrics and alerts.

    ### Act 5 — Next steps
    Expand domains, enrich telemetry, and open SDKs for third‑party integrations.
  </section>

    ## Highlights

    * **Modular & Scalable Architecture:** Domain/module organization enabling independent evolution, reuse, and easier maintenance.
    * **Code Splitting:** On-demand loading improves TTFB and initial interaction on critical routes.
    * **DRY Principle:** Shared components and utilities reduce duplication and defects.
    * **Visual Evolution:** Custom components and pages focused on UX and consistency.
    * **Observability:** Instrumentation for monitoring and diagnostics.
    * **Matrix CI/CD:** Pipelines across multiple Node versions and environments, with Slack notifications.
    * **Dependency Automation:** Automated flows to keep the stack safely up-to-date.
    * **Geolocation (Haversine):** Finds nearby points using the Haversine formula with lat/long distances.

    ## From web‑game curiosity to practicing algorithms

    ### Act 1 — The spark
    Native web games always fascinated me. To exercise **programming logic** and **algorithms**, I turned a classic into a lab: tic‑tac‑toe.

    <Callout type="warning" title="Personal challenge">
      Build a simple yet convincing AI that plays well and reacts fast — no external engines.
    </Callout>

    ### Act 2 — The idea
    Use clear heuristics (block win, look for win, prioritize center/corners) and keep a minimalist, responsive UI.

    ### Act 3 — The experiment
    Implemented with **React** + **TypeScript** and **Tailwind CSS**, UI using **Radix UI** and **Lucide** icons. **State management** uses **Zustand**, structuring move history and a toggleable AI mode.

    ### Act 4 — What I learned
    - Lean, predictable state modeling
    - Separation of rules (game logic) from rendering
    - Simple heuristics can deliver a fun AI for casual games
